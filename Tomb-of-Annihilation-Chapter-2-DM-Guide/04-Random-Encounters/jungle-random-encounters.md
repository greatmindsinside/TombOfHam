## Encounter Table (Roll 1d12)

1. **Dinosaur Herd** (herbivores) - Avoidable, or potential food source
2. **Predator Attack** (carnivorous dinosaur) - Combat encounter
3. **Undead** (zombies/skeletons) - Combat, suggests Soulmonger's influence
4. **Weather Event** (storm, heat wave) - Survival challenge
5. **Disease Vector** (mosquitoes, contaminated water) - Disease check
6. **Discovery** (ruins, trail, camp) - Exploration opportunity
7. **Tribal Encounter** (Batiri goblins, Chultan natives) - Social/combat
8. **Natural Hazard** (quicksand, falling tree, flash flood) - Skill challenge
9. **Other Adventurers** (friendly or hostile) - Social encounter
10. **Magical Anomaly** (strange magic, portal, artifact) - Mystery/opportunity
11. **Animal Attack** (giant snakes, spiders, etc.) - Combat
12. **No Encounter** - Peaceful travel day

## Encounter Details

### 1. Dinosaur Herd

**Type:** Herbivorous dinosaurs (triceratops, hadrosaurs)

**What to Say:**

> "A herd of [dinosaur type] grazes peacefully ahead. The massive creatures pay you no mind, focused on feeding. Your guide signals for caution. 'Herbivores. Usually not aggressive unless threatened. But they're big, and if we startle them, they could stampede. Let's go around quietly.'"

**Behavior:** Generally avoidable, but may charge if threatened

**Creatures:** See [Dinosaur Encounters](./Dinosaur-Encounters.md) for detailed stat blocks

**Opportunities:**

- Potential food source (Survival DC 12 to hunt safely)
- Can observe from distance (Nature DC 10 to identify species)
- May lead to water source

**Combat:** Only if party attacks or gets too close

**Loot:** Meat (1d4 days of rations if successful hunt), hide (20-50 gp), teeth/horns (5-15 gp each)

**Consequences:**

- **If Startled:** Stampede (DC 15 DEX save or 3d10 bludgeoning damage)
- **If Hunted:** Combat with 1-3 dinosaurs
- **Time Lost:** 1-2 hours if hunting

### 2. Predator Attack

**Type:** Carnivorous dinosaur (deinonychus, tyrannosaurus, etc.)

**What to Say:**

> "Your guide freezes, eyes wide with alarm. Ahead, a [predator type] has spotted you. The massive predator's head turns, its eyes locking onto your group. There's no mistaking the intent - this is a hunter, and you're the prey. Your guide's voice is urgent: 'Run. Now. Or fight, but don't hesitate.'"

**Behavior:** Aggressive, hunting the party

**Creatures:** See [Dinosaur Encounters](./Dinosaur-Encounters.md) for detailed stat blocks (T. rex, Velociraptor, Deinonychus, Allosaurus)

**Combat:** Initiative immediately

**Tactics:**

- Ambush from cover
- Target weakest-looking party member
- Retreat if seriously wounded (below 25% HP)

**Skill Checks:**

- **DC 14 Survival**: Find escape route
- **DC 15 Stealth**: Hide successfully
- **DC 12 DEX Save**: Avoid being caught if running

**Loot:** Hide (30-100 gp), teeth/claws (10-25 gp each), meat (1d6 + 2 days rations), trophy (50-200 gp)

**Consequences:**

- **High Damage Risk:** T. rex can one-shot weaker characters
- **Resource Use:** Spells, arrows, hit points, healing potions
- **Time Lost:** 30 minutes to 2 hours for combat and recovery

### 3. Undead

**Type:** Zombies, skeletons, or ghouls

**What to Say (Zombie Horde):**

> "The stench hits you first - decay and death. Then you see them: a shambling horde of zombies, their flesh rotting, their movements jerky and unnatural. They haven't noticed you yet, but they're moving with purpose - all in the same direction, deeper into the jungle. Your guide's expression is grim. 'Undead. They're everywhere now, all moving toward something. The Soulmonger draws them.'"

**Behavior:** Drawn toward Soulmonger, attack living creatures

**Creatures:** See [Undead Encounters](./Undead-Encounters.md) for detailed stat blocks (Zombie, Skeleton, Ghoul, Wight)

**Combat:** Initiative immediately (if detected)

**Significance:** Suggests proximity to Soulmonger or undead source

**Tactics:**

- Mindless aggression (zombies)
- Tactical fighting (skeletons)
- Paralysis attacks (ghouls)
- Life drain (wights)
- No fear, no retreat

**Skill Checks:**

- **DC 12 Stealth**: Hide from undead
- **DC 11 Survival**: Track undead movement direction
- **DC 13 Religion/Arcana**: Understand Soulmonger influence

**Loot:** Usually nothing, but may have clues about source. Skeletons may have usable equipment (1d4 weapons, armor scraps)

**Consequences:**

- **Undead Fortitude:** Zombies are hard to kill
- **Paralysis Risk:** Ghouls can paralyze characters
- **Life Drain:** Wights reduce max HP
- **Directional Clue:** Undead movement indicates direction toward Omu

### 4. Weather Event

**Type:** Tropical storm, extreme heat, or flash flood

**Challenge:** Survival skill challenge

**Effects:**

- **Storm**: Difficult travel, risk to supplies, need shelter
- **Heat**: Exhaustion risk, extra water needed
- **Flood**: Dangerous crossing, may lose supplies

**Resolution:**

- Survival DC 12 to find shelter/avoid worst
- Constitution saves vs. exhaustion
- May lose supplies if failed

---

### 5. Disease Vector

**Type:** Mosquitoes, contaminated water, or infected area

**Challenge:** Disease check

**Effects:**

- DC 10 Constitution save
- Failure: Contract disease (see [Jungle Travel](../02-Travel-Mechanics/jungle-travel.md#disease) | [Disease Mechanics](../05-Weather-and-Survival/Disease-Mechanics.md))

**Prevention:**

- Insect repellent (automatic success)
- Purify water (Survival DC 12 or magic)
- Avoid area (Perception DC 12 to notice danger)

---

### 6. Discovery

**Type:** Ruins, old trail, abandoned camp, or water source

**Opportunity:** Exploration or information

**What to Say:**

> "Through the dense vegetation, you spot [discovery]. It looks [age/condition]. Your guide pauses. 'This is interesting. We should investigate, but be careful.'"

**Possible Discoveries:**

- Ancient ruins (may contain clues or treasure)
- Old expedition trail (may lead to location)
- Abandoned camp (may have supplies or clues)
- Water source (refill supplies)

**Investigation:**

- DC 12 Investigation to find useful items/information
- DC 15 to find hidden secrets
- May trigger additional encounters

### 7. Tribal Encounter

**Type:** Batiri goblins, Chultan natives, or grungs

**What to Say (Batiri Goblins):**

> "You hear chattering and the sound of many small feet. Through the trees, you see a group of goblins - Batiri, your guide identifies them. They're setting up an ambush, clearly planning to attack. But they haven't noticed you yet - you have a moment to decide: fight, negotiate, or avoid."

**What to Say (Chultan Natives):**

> "A group of Chultan natives emerges from the jungle. They're armed but not immediately hostile - they're assessing you, just as you're assessing them. Your guide steps forward, speaking in a language you don't understand. After a moment, they respond. 'They want to know why we're here,' your guide translates. 'This is their territory.'"

**Behavior:** Varies - may be friendly, neutral, or hostile

**Creatures:** See [Tribal Encounters](./Tribal-Encounters.md) for detailed stat blocks (Batiri Goblin, Chultan Warrior, Grung)

**Initial Reaction:**

- Roll 1d6: 1-2 = Hostile, 3-4 = Neutral, 5-6 = Friendly
- Can be modified by party actions

**Social Encounter:**

- **DC 12 Persuasion**: Make friendly
- **DC 10 Persuasion (with trade goods)**: Successfully trade
- **DC 13 Intimidation**: Scare goblins into backing down
- May trade information or supplies
- May offer directions or warnings

**Combat (if hostile):**

- Use appropriate stat blocks
- Goblins: 4-12 in group, cowardly, use traps
- Natives: 3-8 warriors, tactical fighters
- Grungs: 3-8 in pack, poison attacks
- May call for reinforcements
- May retreat if outmatched

**Loot:** Varies - may have information, supplies, or treasure. Goblins: 2d6 × 10 sp, 1d4 × 10 gp. Natives: May trade for information/maps.

**Consequences:**

- **Reputation:** Actions affect future tribal encounters
- **Allies/Enemies:** May gain allies or create enemies
- **Information:** May learn about nearby dangers or locations

### 8. Natural Hazard

**Type:** Quicksand, falling tree, flash flood, or poisonous plants

**What to Say (Quicksand):**

> "The ground gives way beneath [character's] feet. What looked like solid earth is actually quicksand, and they're sinking fast. The more they struggle, the faster they sink. You need to act quickly."

**What to Say (Flash Flood):**

> "A distant roar grows louder. Your guide's eyes widen. 'Flash flood! The storm upstream - the water's coming! Get to high ground, now!'"

**What to Say (Falling Tree):**

> "A massive tree, weakened by rot and storms, begins to fall. It's coming down right where you're standing. You have seconds to react."

**Challenge:** Skill challenge or saving throw

**Detailed Mechanics:** See [Natural Hazards](./Natural-Hazards.md) for full details

**Examples:**

- **Quicksand**: DC 12 Strength (Athletics) to rescue, DC 15 Perception to notice
- **Falling Tree**: DC 13 DEX save (if warned), 3d6 bludgeoning damage if hit
- **Flash Flood**: DC 12 DEX save to reach high ground, 2d6 bludgeoning if caught
- **Poisonous Plants**: DC 12-14 CON save, poisoned condition, ongoing damage

**Resolution:**

- Success: Avoid or escape hazard
- Failure: Take damage, lose supplies, or waste time

**Consequences:**

- **Quicksand:** Equipment loss (25% chance, 1d4 items), exhaustion
- **Flash Flood:** Equipment loss (50% chance, 1d6 items), drowning risk
- **Falling Tree:** 3d6 damage, may be pinned (1d6 damage/round)
- **Poisonous Plants:** Poisoned condition, ongoing damage, weakness

### 9. Other Adventurers

**Type:** Friendly expedition, hostile treasure hunters, or lost travelers

**What to Say (Friendly Expedition):**

> "You spot another group of adventurers ahead - they look like explorers, not hostile. They notice you and wave, calling out in Common. 'Hello there! We're from [location]. Are you heading to [destination] as well?'"

**What to Say (Hostile Treasure Hunters):**

> "A group of armed adventurers blocks your path. They're well-equipped and look dangerous. Their leader steps forward, hand on weapon. 'This area is ours. Turn around, or we'll make you.'"

**What to Say (Lost Travelers):**

> "You find a small group of travelers, clearly lost and desperate. They're low on supplies and look exhausted. One of them approaches you: 'Please, can you help us? We've been lost for days, and we don't know which way to go.'"

**Behavior:** Varies based on type

**NPC Stat Blocks (if combat):**

- **Treasure Hunter:** AC 14 (leather armor), HP 22 (4d8 + 4), +5 to hit, 7 (1d8 + 3) damage
- **Lost Traveler:** AC 11, HP 8 (1d8), non-combatant

**Friendly:**

- May share information about area
- May trade supplies
- May offer to travel together temporarily
- **DC 12 Persuasion**: Gain useful information

**Hostile:**

- May try to rob party
- May compete for same goals
- Combat if party doesn't back down
- **DC 13 Intimidation**: Scare them off
- **DC 15 Persuasion**: Negotiate peaceful resolution

**Lost Travelers:**

- May need help (food, water, directions)
- May have useful information about dangers ahead
- May become quest hook
- **Help Reward:** Information, gratitude, potential future aid

**Loot:** Hostile adventurers may have treasure (2d6 × 10 gp, equipment). Friendly/lost travelers have little to offer.

**Consequences:**

- **Friendly:** Positive relationship, information network
- **Hostile:** Combat risk, reputation impact
- **Lost Travelers:** Time lost helping, but may gain information

### 10. Magical Anomaly

**Type:** Strange magic, portal, artifact, or magical effect

**Mystery:** Requires investigation

**What to Say:**

> "The air shimmers with strange energy. Magic feels wrong here—distorted, twisted. Your guide stops, looking nervous. 'This is not natural. Something powerful happened here, or is happening still.'"

**Investigation:**

- Arcana DC 15 to understand the magic
- May be related to Soulmonger
- May be portal or teleportation effect
- May contain clues or treasure

**Danger:** May have magical traps or effects

### 11. Animal Attack

**Type:** Giant snakes, spiders, or other dangerous animals

**What to Say (Giant Snake):**

> "Something massive moves in the undergrowth ahead - something long and sinuous. Then you see it: a giant constrictor snake, easily 30 feet long, its eyes fixed on you. Your guide hisses: 'Giant snake. They're ambush predators. Don't let it wrap around you - it'll crush you to death.'"

**What to Say (Giant Spider):**

> "Webs stretch between the trees ahead, and you see something large moving in the shadows. A giant spider, the size of a horse, drops down from above. Your guide shouts: 'Spider! Watch out for its web - if it catches you, you're done!'"

**Combat:** Initiative immediately

**Creatures:**

#### Giant Constrictor Snake

**Brief Stat Block:**

- **AC:** 12
- **HP:** 60 (8d12 + 8)
- **Speed:** 30 ft., swim 30 ft.
- **STR:** 15 (+2), **DEX:** 14 (+2), **CON:** 13 (+1), **INT:** 1 (-5), **WIS:** 10 (+0), **CHA:** 3 (-4)
- **Senses:** Blindsight 10 ft., Passive Perception 10
- **Challenge:** 2 (450 XP)

**Actions:**

- **Bite:** +6 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 8 (1d8 + 4) piercing damage.
- **Constrict:** +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 13 (2d8 + 4) bludgeoning damage, and the target is grappled (escape DC 14). Until this grapple ends, the creature is restrained, and the snake can't constrict another target.

#### Giant Spider

**Brief Stat Block:**

- **AC:** 14 (natural armor)
- **HP:** 26 (4d8 + 8)
- **Speed:** 30 ft., climb 30 ft.
- **STR:** 14 (+2), **DEX:** 16 (+3), **CON:** 12 (+1), **INT:** 2 (-4), **WIS:** 11 (+0), **CHA:** 4 (-3)
- **Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft., Passive Perception 10
- **Challenge:** 1 (200 XP)

**Actions:**

- **Bite:** +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 7 (1d8 + 3) piercing damage, and the target must make a DC 11 Constitution saving throw, taking 9 (2d8) poison damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.

**Special Abilities:**

- **Spider Climb:** The spider can climb difficult surfaces, including upside down on ceilings, without needing to make an ability check.
- **Web Sense:** While in contact with a web, the spider knows the exact location of any other creature in contact with the same web.
- **Web Walker:** The spider ignores movement restrictions caused by webbing.

**Tactics:**

- Ambush from cover
- Use environment (trees, water, etc.)
- May have pack tactics
- Constrictor snakes try to grapple and constrict
- Spiders use webs to restrain targets

**Skill Checks:**

- **DC 13 Perception**: Notice animal before it attacks
- **DC 12 Nature**: Identify animal type and weaknesses
- **DC 14 DEX Save**: Avoid constrictor's grapple
- **DC 11 CON Save**: Resist spider poison

**Loot:** Hide (10-30 gp), venom (50-100 gp to alchemists), meat (1d4 days rations)

**Consequences:**

- **Constrictor:** Grappled and restrained, ongoing damage
- **Spider:** Poisoned condition, webbed and restrained
- **Resource Use:** Spells, arrows, hit points
- **Time Lost:** 30 minutes to 1 hour for combat

### 12. No Encounter

**Peaceful travel day** - No major events

**What to Say:**

> "The day passes relatively uneventfully. The jungle is still dangerous, but today you avoid the worst of it. Your guide navigates skillfully, and you make good progress."

**Still roll for:**

- Weather
- Disease (if applicable)
- Navigation

## Customizing Encounters

**For Story Needs:**

- Choose encounters that advance the plot
- Use encounters to provide information
- Connect encounters to player backstories
- Vary encounter types (not all combat)

**For Pacing:**

- Use peaceful days to give players a break
- Use discovery encounters to build excitement
- Use combat encounters to maintain tension
- Use social encounters to provide information
