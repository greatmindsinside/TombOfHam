# Exhaustion Management

*Official source: Exhaustion rules: **pp. 42-43***  
*Reference: [D&D 5E - Tomb of Annihilation.pdf](../../D&D%205E%20-%20Tomb%20of%20Annihilation.pdf)*

**Location:** Jungle (Daily Exhaustion Checks)

## Overview

Exhaustion is a constant threat in the jungle. Heat, forced march, disease, and lack of resources can all cause exhaustion levels that weaken and potentially kill players.

## Causes of Exhaustion

### Extreme Heat

- **DC 10 Constitution Save** (daily in extreme heat)
- **Failure**: Gain 1 level of exhaustion
- **Prevention**: Adequate water, shade, rest

### Forced March

- **Traveling more than 8 hours**: DC 10 Constitution Save
- **Failure**: Gain 1 level of exhaustion
- **Prevention**: Normal travel pace, adequate rest

### Disease

- **Disease progression**: Can cause exhaustion
- **Prevention**: Disease treatment, insect repellent

### Lack of Resources

- **No water**: Gain 1 level of exhaustion per day
- **No food**: Gain 1 level of exhaustion after 3 days
- **Prevention**: Adequate supplies

## Exhaustion Levels

### Level 1: Disadvantage on Ability Checks

**What to Say:**

> "The heat and exertion are taking their toll. You're tired, and it's affecting your performance. Simple tasks become more difficult, and you find yourself making mistakes you wouldn't normally make."

**Effects:**

- Disadvantage on all ability checks
- No other penalties yet

### Level 2: Speed Halved

**What to Say:**

> "You're exhausted. Every step is a struggle, and you can't keep up with the group. Your speed is reduced, and you're falling behind. You need rest, or this will get worse."

**Effects:**

- Disadvantage on ability checks
- Speed halved

### Level 3: Disadvantage on Attack Rolls and Saving Throws

**What to Say:**

> "Exhaustion is overwhelming you. You can barely fight, and your defenses are weakened. You're a liability to the group, and you need immediate rest and recovery."

**Effects:**

- Disadvantage on ability checks
- Speed halved
- Disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws

### Level 4: Hit Point Maximum Halved

**What to Say:**

> "You're at your breaking point. Your body is failing, and your maximum hit points are reduced. You're in serious danger, and you need rest immediately."

**Effects:**

- All previous effects
- Hit point maximum halved

### Level 5: Speed Reduced to 0

**What to Say:**

> "You collapse. You can't move, you can't fight, you can barely think. You're completely incapacitated by exhaustion. Without immediate help, you'll die."

**Effects:**

- All previous effects
- Speed reduced to 0

### Level 6: Death

**What to Say:**

> "Exhaustion claims you. Your body gives out, and you die from the strain. The jungle has claimed another victim."

**Effects:**

- Death

## Recovery from Exhaustion

### Short Rest

- **Recovery**: Remove 1 level of exhaustion (if safe)
- **Requires**: 1 hour of rest in safe location

### Long Rest

- **Recovery**: Remove 1 level of exhaustion
- **Requires**: 8 hours of rest in safe location
- **Note**: Cannot recover if still exposed to exhaustion causes

### Medical Treatment

- **Medicine Check (DC 15)**: Remove 1 level of exhaustion
- **Lesser Restoration**: Remove 1 level of exhaustion
- **Greater Restoration**: Remove all exhaustion

## What to Say (Exhaustion Check)

> "The [heat/forced march/disease/lack of resources] is taking its toll. You must make a Constitution save to resist exhaustion. [If failed]: You gain a level of exhaustion. [Describe effects based on level]. You need rest, or this will get worse."
