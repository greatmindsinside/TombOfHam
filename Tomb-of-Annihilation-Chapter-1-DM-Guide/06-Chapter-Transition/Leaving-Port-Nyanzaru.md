# Leaving Port Nyanzaru

*Official source: Chapter 2 start: **p. 37***  
*Reference: [D&D 5E - Tomb of Annihilation.pdf](../../D&D%205E%20-%20Tomb%20of%20Annihilation.pdf)*

## Transition Scene

Once players are ready to depart, describe their exit from the city and entry into the jungle.

**Location:** City Gates, Port Nyanzaru

## What to Say

> "As you and your guide lead the expedition through the massive gates of Port Nyanzaru, the city bustle fades behind you. The jungle looms ahead - a vast, green wall of vegetation that seems to swallow the horizon. Strange calls echo from the canopy, and the air grows heavier with humidity and the scent of decay.
>
> Your guide pauses at the edge of the trail. 'This is where civilization ends,' they say grimly. 'Stay close, watch your step, and remember: everything in the jungle wants to eat you.'
>
> With that sobering thought, you step into the Land of Chult..."

## What Players Experience

- The transition from safe city to dangerous wilderness
- The massive gates of Port Nyanzaru closing behind them
- The jungle looming ahead as an impenetrable green wall
- Strange sounds and scents from the untamed wilderness
- The guide's warning about jungle dangers
- The finality of leaving civilization behind

## Immediate Options for Players

1. **Observe the jungle edge** - Use Perception to spot immediate dangers
2. **Assess readiness** - Use Survival/Investigation to verify preparation
3. **Understand the environment** - Use Nature checks to identify threats
4. **Take final precautions** - Make any last-minute adjustments
5. **Follow the guide** - Trust the guide's expertise and begin the journey
6. **Begin exploration** - Step into the jungle and start Chapter 2

## DM Guidance

This is a major mood shift from safe city to dangerous wilderness. Emphasize the transition and build tension. The guide's warning sets the tone for what's to come. Use skill checks to help players orient themselves and prepare. This marks the end of Chapter 1 - players are now committed to the jungle journey. Transition smoothly to Chapter 2's wilderness exploration. Build excitement and anticipation for the adventures ahead. Mood shift from safe city to dangerous wilderness. Final preparations - last chance for forgotten items. Guide role - let the guide take point in descriptions. Anticipation - build excitement for jungle adventures.

## Complete Essential Information Delivery

If players need all essential information about leaving Port Nyanzaru at once, use this comprehensive delivery:

**What to Say (Complete Essential Info):**

> "As you approach the massive gates of Port Nyanzaru, this is what you need to understand about leaving:
>
> **First, this is the point of no return:** Once you step through these gates, you're committed to the jungle journey. The city will be behind you, and the wilderness will be ahead. This marks the end of Chapter 1 and the beginning of Chapter 2. Make sure you're ready.
>
> **Second, what awaits you:** The jungle is a vast, green expanse that has claimed the lives of countless explorers. Strange calls echo from the canopy. The air grows heavier with humidity and the scent of decay. Everything in the jungle wants to eat you. Disease, weather, creatures, getting lost, exhaustion - these are daily threats. The jungle doesn't care how prepared you think you are - it will test you every single day.
>
> **Third, your guide is essential:** Your guide knows the terrain, the dangers, and how to survive. Trust them. Follow their lead. They've survived multiple expeditions, and their knowledge is your best defense. Without them, you'll get lost and die. With them, you have a chance.
>
> **Fourth, final preparations:** This is your last chance to check supplies, adjust gear, or make any final arrangements. Once you leave, you're committed. Use skill checks to verify readiness - Perception to spot immediate dangers, Investigation to verify preparation, Survival to assess jungle readiness, and Insight to sense if you're truly ready.
>
> **Fifth, what you should have:** You need a guide (essential), supplies (essential), a solid lead pointing to a destination (essential), and understanding of jungle dangers (important). Optional but recommended: charter, faction contacts, side quest completion, and a specific destination chosen.
>
> **Sixth, the transition:** This is a major mood shift from safe city to dangerous wilderness. The guide's warning sets the tone. The jungle looms ahead - a wall of green that seems to stretch forever. This is where civilization ends, and survival begins.
>
> The gates are opening. The jungle waits. Are you ready?'"

**When to Use This:** If players are at the gates but need comprehensive information, if they're uncertain about readiness, or when doing a final transition check

## Skill Checks

### Perception/Investigation Checks

**DC 12 Perception Check**: Observe the jungle edge and spot immediate dangers

- **Success**: "You notice details about the jungle edge: signs of recent animal activity, potential hazards, and the transition from civilization to wilderness. You can identify immediate dangers and prepare accordingly."
- **Failure**: "The jungle appears dense and mysterious, but you can't identify specific dangers or hazards at the edge."
- **Critical Success (17+)**: "Your observation is comprehensive: you can identify specific dangers (predator tracks, poisonous plants, unstable terrain), spot signs of recent activity (other expeditions, animal movements), and even notice potential advantages (safe paths, water sources, shelter). You're well-prepared for the transition into the jungle."

**DC 13 Investigation Check**: Assess the trail and verify readiness

- **Success**: "By examining the trail and your preparations, you can verify that you're ready for the journey. You can identify any last-minute concerns or missing items, and ensure your guide is prepared."
- **Failure**: "Everything seems ready, but you can't be certain if there are any concerns or missing elements."
- **Critical Success (18+)**: "Your investigation is thorough: you can verify all aspects of your readiness, identify any last-minute concerns or missing items, spot potential problems with the trail or your guide, and even find ways to improve your chances of success. You're confident you're as prepared as possible."

### Additional Skill Checks

**DC 12 Survival Check**: Assess readiness for jungle travel and identify immediate needs

- **Success**: "Your survival knowledge helps you assess your readiness for jungle travel. You can identify if you have the right supplies, if your guide is capable, and if you're prepared for the specific challenges of Chult's jungle. You can identify immediate needs or concerns."
- **Failure**: "You think you're ready, but you can't be certain if you're fully prepared for jungle travel."
- **Critical Success (17+)**: "Your survival assessment is comprehensive: you can verify all aspects of jungle readiness, identify any missing supplies or preparation, spot potential problems with your gear or guide, and even find ways to improve your chances of survival. You're confident you're ready for the journey and can identify immediate priorities."

**DC 11 Nature Check**: Understand the jungle environment and its dangers

- **Success**: "Your knowledge of nature helps you understand the jungle environment. You can identify dangerous plants and animals, understand the ecosystem, and prepare for the specific challenges of tropical jungle travel."
- **Failure**: "The jungle is unfamiliar, and you can't determine the specific dangers or challenges."
- **Critical Success (16+)**: "Your nature knowledge is extensive: you can identify specific dangerous species, understand the jungle ecosystem, predict potential threats, and even find ways to use the environment to your advantage. You're well-prepared for the jungle's challenges."

**DC 10 Insight Check**: Sense if you're truly ready or rushing

- **Success**: "You can sense if you're truly ready for the journey or if you're rushing into danger. You can identify if you need more preparation, if your guide is reliable, and if you have the necessary knowledge and resources."
- **Failure**: "You feel ready, but you can't be certain if you're rushing or if you need more preparation."
- **Critical Success (15+)**: "Your insight is sharp: you can accurately assess your readiness, identify any concerns or gaps, sense if you're rushing or if you're truly prepared, and even predict potential problems. You can make an informed decision about whether to proceed or prepare more."

## Encounter: The Final Gate

Use this encounter when players are ready to leave Port Nyanzaru and approach the city gates for the final time.

**Location:** City Gates, Port Nyanzaru

### Encounter Setup

Players approach the massive gates of Port Nyanzaru with their guide and supplies, ready to begin their journey into the jungle.

### Encounter Dialogue

> "The massive gates of Port Nyanzaru loom before you, the last barrier between civilization and the untamed jungle beyond. City guards stand watch, their expressions grim as they observe expeditions preparing to depart. You can see other groups making final preparations - checking supplies, saying farewells, or simply steeling themselves for what lies ahead.
>
> As you approach, a guard captain steps forward. 'Another expedition heading into the jungle,' he says, his tone matter-of-fact but not unkind. 'I've seen hundreds of you come through these gates. Some return. Most don't. The jungle doesn't care how prepared you think you are - it will test you every single day.'
>
> He looks you over, assessing your gear and your guide. 'You look better prepared than most. But remember: the jungle is always hungry. Stay alert, trust your guide, and don't take unnecessary risks. Good luck - you'll need it.'
>
> With that, he gestures to the guards, and the massive gates begin to creak open. Beyond, the jungle waits - a wall of green that seems to stretch forever. Your guide takes a deep breath and steps forward. 'This is it,' they say. 'No turning back now. Let's go.'"

### Encounter Options

1. **Take final assessment** - Use skill checks to verify readiness one last time
2. **Say farewells** - Interact with guards or other departing expeditions
3. **Make final preparations** - Check supplies, adjust gear, take last-minute actions
4. **Observe the jungle** - Use Perception to spot immediate dangers
5. **Follow the guide** - Trust the guide and step through the gates
6. **Turn back** - Decide to return to the city for more preparation (if needed)

### Encounter Skill Checks

**DC 12 Perception Check**: Observe the jungle edge and spot immediate dangers

- **Success**: "As the gates open, you can see the jungle edge clearly. You notice signs of recent activity - tracks, disturbed vegetation, perhaps even the remains of a previous expedition's camp. You can identify immediate dangers and prepare accordingly."
- **Failure**: "The jungle appears as an impenetrable wall of green, and you can't identify specific dangers or hazards."
- **Critical Success (17+)**: "Your observation is comprehensive: you can identify specific dangers (predator tracks, poisonous plants, unstable terrain), spot signs of recent expeditions (abandoned camps, trails), notice potential advantages (safer paths, water sources), and even detect signs of what lies ahead. You're well-prepared for the transition."

**DC 13 Investigation Check**: Final readiness check before departure

- **Success**: "You do a final check of your preparations. You verify you have all essential supplies, your guide is ready, and you're prepared for the journey. You can identify any last-minute concerns."
- **Failure**: "Everything seems ready, but you can't be certain if there are any final concerns or missing elements."
- **Critical Success (18+)**: "Your final check is thorough: you verify all aspects of readiness, identify any last-minute concerns, spot potential problems, and even find ways to improve your chances. You're confident you're as prepared as possible and ready to face whatever the jungle throws at you."

**DC 12 Insight Check**: Sense if you're truly ready or if something feels wrong

- **Success**: "You can sense if you're truly ready for the journey or if something feels off. You can identify if you need more preparation, if your guide is reliable, or if there are any concerns you should address before leaving."
- **Failure**: "You feel ready, but you can't be certain if you're rushing or if there are concerns you should address."
- **Critical Success (17+)**: "Your insight is sharp: you can accurately assess your readiness, identify any concerns or gaps, sense if you're rushing, and even predict potential problems. You can make an informed decision about whether to proceed or prepare more."

### Reward

- **Confidence** - Verification of readiness (if successful checks)
- **Information** - Insights about immediate dangers or advantages
- **Final Warnings** - Guard's advice about jungle dangers
- **Story Progression** - Marks the transition to Chapter 2

### Using This Encounter

- **If players are ready to leave**: Final encounter before Chapter 2 begins
- **If players need final verification**: Skill checks confirm readiness
- **If players are uncertain**: Provides opportunity for final assessment
- **If players want to turn back**: Allows for last-minute return to city
