# Alternative Chapter Structures

*Official source: Chapter 1: **pp. 15-36***  
*Reference: [D&D 5E - Tomb of Annihilation.pdf](../../D&D%205E%20-%20Tomb%20of%20Annihilation.pdf)*

**Note:** This file contains alternative structures and DM techniques that are not from the official source material. These are tools to help DMs adapt the campaign to different play styles and time constraints.

## Fast-Track Version

- Quick landing zone: one arrival scene, one social scene, one street encounter
- Pick guide, buy gear, enter jungle by session's end
- **See**: [Fast-Track-Chapter-1.md](./Fast-Track-Chapter-1.md) for complete condensed guide
- **See**: [Fast-Track-Chapter-2.md](./Fast-Track-Chapter-2.md) for wilderness exploration fast-track

## City-Campaign Mini Arc

- Extend over multiple sessions for politics-focused players
- Emphasize Merchant Prince rivalries, criminal underworld, dinosaur racing leagues, faction missions

## Tutorial Mode

- Teach new players core mechanics:
  - Skill checks and social interaction
  - Light combat/chases in streets
  - Travel prep and resource management

## Customizing for Your Group

- **Fast Group**: Use fast-track version
- **Roleplaying Focused**: Extend city exploration
- **Combat Heavy**: Add more street encounters
- **Politics Interested**: Include faction intrigue
- **New Players**: Use tutorial mode emphasis

## When to Use Alternatives

- **Time Constraints**: Fast-track to get to jungle quicker
- **Player Preferences**: Extend based on what they enjoy
- **Campaign Style**: Adapt to your group's play style
- **Session Length**: Short sessions may need faster pace
